Nigeria Changed the Rules on Invoicing. Here's What That Means for You.

The government now requires every business invoice to go through a digital validation system before it reaches your client.

If your invoices don't have a valid Invoice Reference Number (IRN) and aren't validated against the national e-invoicing standard, they get rejected — and your client can't pay you until it's fixed.

It's not complicated. But it does require the right tool. That's what Zutax is.

⚠️ What's Now Required — in Plain English
🔢
Every invoice needs an IRN

An Invoice Reference Number, issued by the system, must appear on every invoice you send to clients.

✅
Invoices must be digitally validated

The invoice must match the national NRS format — correct tax codes, mandatory fields, proper structure.

🔏
It must be signed before it's sent

requires a digital signature on every invoice before it reaches your client.
